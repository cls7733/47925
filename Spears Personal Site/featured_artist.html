Bob Marley was born Robert Nesta Marley on February 6, 1945. Bob was born to Cedella Marley. Bob Marley's abundant musical talents were nurtured while he lived in Kingston, Jamaica. In 1963 Bob Marley and his childhood friend Neville Livingston a.k.a. Bunny Wailer began attending vocal classes held by Trench Town. A lifelong source of inspiration, Bob immortalized Trench Town in his songs "No Woman No Cry" (1974), "Trench Town Rock" (1975) and "Trench Town", the latter released posthumously in 1983. In his Trench Town yard, Higgs introduced Bob and Bunny to Peter Macintosh Tosh and The Bob Marley and The Wailers legend was born. While with the Wailers Bob Marley made hits like "Get Up Stand Up," "Stir It Up," "Natural Mystic," "Natty Dread" and "I shot the Sheriff" just to name a few. The Bob Marley influence upon various populations remains unparalleled, irrespective of race, color or creed. Bob Marley's revolutionary yet unifying music, challenging colonialism, racism, "fighting against ism and scism" as he sang in "One Drop. “Even though he is not with us today Bob Marley's music and Legend will live on forever.

Anthony Moses Davis (August 22, 1973), better known by his stage name Beenie Man is a Grammy Award winning Reggae Artist. Davis was born in the Waterhouse district of Kingston in 1973. He was involved in the music industry from a young age, starting toasting at the age of five, and was encouraged by his uncle Sydney Knowles, who played drums for Jimmy Cliff. He had hits in the UK in 1998 with "Who am I" #10, in 2003 with "Street Life" #13 and "Feel it Boy" UK #9, a duet with Janet Jackson, and in 2004 with "Dude" #7 and "King of the Dancehall" #14. He was also a judge for the 6th annual Independent Music Awards to support independent artists' careers. In April 2008 it was announced that Beenie Man was to co-write and star in the film Kingston. In October 2010 Beenie Man came out with the EP "I'm Drinking Rum and Red Bull", which included 4 songs, "I’m drinking Rum and Red Bull", "I'm Okay", and two versions of "Stack and Pile". Beenie Man is currently dealing with controversy over his lyrics.

Lady Saw was born Marion Hall on, 12 July 1968. She is a Jamaican Reggae Singer, known as the queen of dancehall. Lady Saw was born in Galina, Saint Mary, Jamaica and began performing with local sound systems at the age of fifteen. She went on to work with the Stereo One system in Kingston. Her performances brought her to the attention of record producers and she recorded her first single for producer Piper, and "Love Me or Lef Me", produced by Olive Shaw received significant airplay. She had her first hit with "If Him Lef" and she topped the Jamaican charts with "Good Man". She soon became known for her slack style, with songs such as "Stab Up De Meat". She is the first female DJ to win a Grammy Award and to be certified as a triple-platinum artist. She is also the first woman to headline dancehall shows outside her native Jamaica. She launched her own record label, Divas Records, in January 2010. In November 2012 she announced that she was turning her back on dancehall and would be concentrating on Gospel music in future.

Bounty Killer was born Rodney Basil Price on 12 June 1972 in Kingston, Jamaica. He is a Grammy nominated Jamaican Reggae and Dancehall DJ. He is the founder of a dancehall collective known as The Alliance. Price moved to Kingston at an early age, His father owned and ran the Black Scorpio sound system and Price started his musical career as a sound system deejay in his early teens. At the age of 14, Price was shot by a stray bullet during a gunfight between rival political factions, and while in hospital he came up with the name Bounty Killer. After recovering, he increased his performances on a greater number of sound systems, and turned his attention towards recording. During the 1990s, Price voiced for several producers and labels in Jamaica, releasing songs such as "Defend the Poor," "Mama," "Book, Book, Book," "Babylon System" and "Down in the Ghetto". At about this time, he became known in USA and in Europe. In 2008, Bounty Killer collaborated with Daddy Yankee in the song "Controlando El Area" for the album "Caribbean Connection".
